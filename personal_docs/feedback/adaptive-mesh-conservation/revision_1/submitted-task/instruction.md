Something in `/app` drives a finite-volume sweep across six bundled scenarios on a grid whose structure changes during the run. The totals are not holding up. The combined picture in `/app/output/conservation_audit.json` drifts away from each scenario's clean starting point under any split or merge of the grid. The same scenario also diverges from its own uninterrupted picture in a run that takes a midway snapshot to `/app/work/vault.bin` and resumes from it. A scenario that only merges cells, with no splitting, drifts as well.

Rebuild with CMake, then re-run `bash /app/scripts/run-matrix.sh` to regenerate the report. Each scenario row carries its `base_total`, its `after_total` at the end of the run, and a `drift_ratio` between them; the remaining columns and the per-scenario helper commands are described in `/app/docs/layout.md` and `/app/docs/operations.md`. The TSV starting states under `/app/data/init` and the schedules in `/app/config/cases.toml` are fixed inputs; do not edit them.

Make the simulation hold its totals through the structural changes and the snapshot round-trip. The regenerated report must agree with an independent recomputation of every scenario, and two clean runs from the same inputs must produce equivalent reports.
